About Me

I am an experimental physicist dedicated to uncovering novel quantum phenomena in condensed matter systems. My research bridges materials synthesis, nanofabrication, and quantum transport measurements to explore emergent properties in topological materials.

"My work focuses on the intersection of topology, symmetry, and electron correlations in quantum materials, with particular emphasis on kagome lattices and altermagnets for next-generation quantum technologies."

Using molecular beam epitaxy and focous ion beam, I synthesize high-quality thin films of intermetallic kagome compounds like FeSn, Fe₃Sn, and Co₃Sn and microstructured Hall bars of altermagnet CrSb. Through advanced nanofabrication techniques, I create devices to probe their quantum transport properties, including nonlinear Hall effects, anomalous in-plane Hall responses, and Berry curvature phenomena at low and room temperatures.
